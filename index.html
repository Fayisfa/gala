Treasure Hunt – Nature’s Secret

I stand in long disciplined rows,
Quiet sentinels of warm and distant lands.
Wanderers don’t seek my fruit or shade,
Yet I’m prized for something that flows when my skin is scored.

What leaves my wound isn’t sap as you know it —
It’s a strange, resilient thread that begins as liquid
And ends as something stretched, shaped, and pulled
Through the hands of the world.

Name me, and move to the next stage.
